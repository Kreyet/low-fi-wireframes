What we do

Commodities trade breaks down where it matters most — at the coordination layer.

The right buyer can't find the right producer. Documentation falls out of alignment at customs. Logistics partners aren't integrated into the transaction flow. Deals stall or collapse before off-take.

Commodities Trade Facilitation

Structured trade from African origin to international off-take.

We facilitate structured commodities trade by linking producers, exporters, buyers, and logistics partners across selected African and international markets. Our role focuses on partner coordination, transaction structuring, documentation alignment, and managing the flow from origin to off-take — working alongside international trade and customs advisors in the Americas and Europe where required.

Structured commodities trade that reduces deal failure, eliminates coordination gaps, and connects African supply to international off-take through a managed, end-to-end facilitation model.

GOC Engagement — Global Opportunities Committee

Institutional market access across 40+ countries.

Dunamis leadership engages international markets through the Global Opportunities Committee (GOC) of the Atlanta Black Chambers — a global business chamber operating across 40+ countries. This network supports partner identification, market access, and deal positioning between African markets, the United States, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia.

Verified institutional reach into international buyer and partner networks across four continents — turning market access from an aspiration into an operational reality.

Results & Outcomes

What changes when commodities trade is properly structured.

Before

Producers and exporters have supply but no reliable route to international buyers. Buyers can't access verified African supply with confidence. Transactions stall on documentation, logistics gaps, and customs complexity.

After
